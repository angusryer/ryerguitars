About Dave

I have always had a deep appreciation for fine woodworking, especially as it applies to musical instruments. In the late 1980s, I repaired stringed instruments for local music shops, and since the turn of the millennium I have dedicated myself to learning the art of crafting high quality acoustic and classical guitars — developing my own voice as a luthier through years of hands-on building. Early in that journey I had the good fortune of training with Sergei de Jonge and spending over two years working in his shop, an experience that helped shape my foundational technique.

I came to realize that there is no better way to live than to do what I have the passion for. Art and the craft of building something so beautifully majestic, rich in detail, technologically advanced and yet, so simple. The guitar, in all of its various forms, has been around for eons — so widely recognized and appreciated the world over — and yet, there's still so much to learn, so many nuances that make each guitar uniquely personal.

Whether it be a classical, flamenco or steel stringed instrument, each will be unique, designed and built according to the desires of the player. Together, we will build a meticulously handcrafted instrument using the finest tone woods and the very best techniques to ensure every client's experience exceeds their expectations.

— David Ryer

Standard Specs

  • Tops — Sitka Spruce, Lutz Spruce, Engelmann, Cedar
  • Backs & Sides — Indian Rosewood or Mahogany
  • Neck — Mahogany, Spanish Cedar
  • Bridge — Rosewood or Ebony
  • Fingerboard — Rosewood or Ebony
  • Scale Length — 61 to 65 cm
  • Tuners — Gotoh 510's or Schertler
  • Nut & Saddle — Bone
  • Finish — French polish or Poly

Available Options

  • Single cutaway
  • Arm relief / Arm rest (Classical)
  • Chest relief
  • Double top
  • Side sound port
  • Left handed
  • K&K Pick-up
  • Snakewood binding, bridge, fingerboard
  • Custom inlay work
Courses

Lutherie Course

$5,000 + tax · up to 200 hours

Students learn to design and build their own quality acoustic guitar. The cost of materials and use of tools is included. Courses typically run Monday to Friday but can be customized to meet your needs. All stages of the building process are covered — from wood selection, carving the neck, bending sides, bracing, tuning tops and backs, binding, finishing, and final assembly and setup. A 20% down payment is required on registration.

Very limited space available at the shop in Madoc, Ontario. Please call or email to arrange.
